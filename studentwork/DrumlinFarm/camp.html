Camp and Staff Credentials

Drumlin Farm Day Camp is an American Camp Association accredited camp and a member of the Massachusetts Camping Association.



At Drumlin Farm, we focus on consistent high quality camp experiences for your son or daughter by adhering to a low camper to staff ratios, a highly trained and experienced staff, and a focus on building experiences that will last a lifetime. Our staff of dedicated teacher naturalists and program directors will make every camper’s experience their top priority. Staffs are carefully selected for their experience and skills working with children and youth in recreational and educational environments; our staff are college age and above. All our staff must pass background checks and are required to attend an eight to ten day training session. Every staff member holds current CPR and First Aid certifications.

Camp Goals

We aim to create an experience that is both educational and fun, and uses the diverse habitats that our sanctuary has to offer. Campers will do farm chores, and learn about agriculture and livestock. They will also learn about various natural habitats, mainly the fields, forests, and ponds and wetlands that dot the sanctuary, doing activities such as ponding, bug catching, bird watching, and log rolling to learn about the various inhabitants of each ecosystem. Our Teacher/Naturalists are all individuals that are excited and enthusiastic about nature and agriculture and are looking to pass that joy on to the younger generation. We feel that we provide a unique camp environment that you cannot find anywhere else.

Our Program

Many of the problems facing today’s society center around the mistreatment of the world’s natural ecosystems. We believe that the best way to combat the wrongs that are being committed is to educate the next generation of the value of the environment, not just areas that are wild but also through responsible agricultural practices. Our camp program balances education and fun, tailoring activities and educational experiences based on the age of each camp group.

Summer at Drumlin Farm is a special time and Drumlin Farm has been offering summer camp programs to children since 1956. The sanctuary fills with the joyous shouts and songs of children, all exploring the diverse habitats that we have to offer. They will do chores on the farm, mucking stalls, weeding, feeding animals, and even milking a cow. They will also adventure into nature, exploring the forest, ponds, and fields looking to discover life. We aim to teach campers about the connectedness of the natural world, and emphasize our place within it as respectful citizens and stewards of the planet.

Older campers will expand their knowledge through programs that focus on specialized knowledge in areas such as conservation, sustainable agriculture, local cultural history, and the habitats of Massachusetts. Like any other summer camp, the day is also filled with songs, stories, games, cooking, campfires, and most of all, fun. Each session there is an all-camp family night that gives parents the chance to meet the staff and experience the joy of camp first hand.

Make your summer a Drumlin summer!



Farm Mania

A one-week farm camp for campers age 4 – 6th grade

Dates
Farm Mania 1 June 25 – June 29
Farm Mania 2 August 13 – August 17

Come to Drumlin Farm for an exciting week of hands-on farm activities! Meet our animals, help care for the sheep and goats, collect chicken eggs, groom the pony, explore the fields and sample fresh vegetables. Pick strawberries and make fresh whipped cream in June, harvest fresh corn or potatoes and make butter in August.

In groups organized by grade, campers will participate in farm chores and learn how to care for the animals around the farm and help the farmers with vegetable planting and harvesting. Learn what it means to be a farmer and experience the behind-the-scenes happenings around the sanctuary! Each day of the camp session will focus on a different farm animal theme; daily activities include farm chores, getting to know the animal, cooking, stories, arts & crafts, games and quiet reflection time. End the week with a special day of Wacky Farm Olympics.

Nature Camp


Dates

Session 2 July 16 – July 27

Our two-week Nature Camp balances farm focused activities and explorations with nature. Campers are encouraged to play, learn, and grow as they observe the natural world of Drumlin Farm. Each day of a camp session will focus on a different farm animal or nature theme.

Daily activities include farm chores, cooking, arts & crafts, nature walks, games, quiet time and nature reflection time. Our goal is to facilitate positive outdoor experiences with nature in the hope that all campers will be comfortable interacting with the natural world throughout their lifetime.

For more details, including a one-week version for some ages running August 13 – August 17, as well as more specific details about each Nature Camp program, please see the camp brochure.

Teen Programs


Programs for teenagers at Drumlin Farm are broken into five groups. All camps run for full days, from 8:30am – 3:00pm.


Counselor In Training (CIT) (entering grades 9 – 12)

Would you like to be a camp counselor or a naturalist? Our CIT program is a leadership program designed for high school students who wish to learn more aobut and gain experience working with children in a natural history and farm-focused camp environment. Our CIT Coordinator provides on-going training and support for CIT’ as they develop individual teaching, leadership, and natural history skills. You’ll learn about the environment, assist counselors, lead group activities, and participate in a conservation project.

Member $400, Non-member $500


Ecology in Action (entering grades 9 – 12)

Are you interested in how conservation organizations conserve habitat for people and wildlife? Would you like to practice field research that helps scientists protect important bird, insect, and amphibian species? Ecology in action is a mix of field science, and research using the tools and skills of conservation biologists including GPS/GIS, advocacy to learn how conservation professionals support environmental legislation, and a virtual internship, at the office of a fictitious urban and regional planning firm. You’ll have a chance to apply your understanding of wildlife and their habitat needs, weigh the trade-offs of land use decisions in ecologically-sensitive areas, interact with stakeholders and use iPlan, a custom-designed Geographic Informaiton System, to develop a land use plan for a local community in our watershed.

Member $275, Non-member $375

Session 1: July 23 – 27. Session 2: August 6 – 10.

9:00am – 4:00pm


Animal Apprentices (entering grades 9 – 12)

Do you know what it’ like to feed, care for, and work with wild and domestic animals? Are you interested in exploring the ecology of a farm and how cultivated farm land and wild habitats are connected? Animal Apprentices is designed for teens who wish to learn more about animals (wild and domestic) and gain experience in their direct care from animal care professionals and naturalists. Working with our farmer and wildlife care staff, particpants will elarn animal science, assist with animal chores, receive training for working with animals, and complete an animal-related stewardship project on the sanctuary. Explore the animals of Drumlin Farm, learn about environmental ethics, and meet other like-minded teens.

Member $400, Non-member $500

Session 1: June 25 – 29. Session 2: August 13 – 17.


Farm-Ecologists (entering grades 8 – 10)

Get a behind-the-scenes view of Drumlin Farm. This internship-style experience will take you to all corners of the farm as you shadow our staff and participate in the many activities that keep Drumlin Farm running. Join our livestock and wildlife care staff in caring for the animals. Learn about the lcoal food movement, how to grow vegetables, ahrvest them and prepare your own lunch. Work with the education department to develop new programs for visitors. Join our ecological management team to research some of the remote habitats of the sanctuary. Each day will be a different adventure, and we’ll leave time for traditional cmap fun along the way.

Member $555, Non-member $655


Avian Adventurers (entering grades 6 – 12)

Embark on a week-long camping and birding adventure. Throughout the week we will learn about birds and their relationships with various habitats in Massachusetts. Through hikes, boat trips, beach trips, guided tours, and our own exploring we will go in search of the best birds that August has to offer. This program will depart Drumlin Farm on Monday morning and return on Friday afternoon. Itinerary and packing lists will be sent to particpants after registration.

Member $575, Non-member $675
