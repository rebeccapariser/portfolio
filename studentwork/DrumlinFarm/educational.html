Our school programs cultivate appreciation and understanding of native plants, animals, and their habitats so we can work together to ensure that they are protected.

For over 50 years Drumlin Farm has provided our educational programs to hundreds of thousands of elementary, middle, and high school students. We offer a curriculum that focuses on Massachusetts’ habitats, and teaches about our interconnection with the land, as well as the natural and cultural history of our state. Read More

On-Site School Programs

On any day, learners can be seen exploring vernal pools, forests, fields, and farmland. On-Site programs take full advantage of the opportunities to learn about the ecology of diverse habitats and the adaptations of the animals and plants that live there.

Ark Outreach Program

Our Ark Outreach program brings our naturalists and native wildlife to schoolyards and neighborhoods throughout the state.

Drumlin Farm’s Community Preschool is open and enrolling children ages 3 – 6.

The Community Preschool program was founded on the idea that play—indoor and outdoor, as well as structured and unstructured—is an essential aspect of children’s learning.

The curriculum is based on the interests of the children, as well as the seasonal happenings on the farm and sanctuary. Students get to experience hands-on activities every day. Read More
